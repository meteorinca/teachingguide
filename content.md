# Six Signposts from Visible Learning for Teachers
### Excellence in Education

The conclusions in *Visible Learning* provide a roadmap for instructional excellence. Here are the six signposts:

1. **The Power of Teachers**
   Teachers are among the most powerful influences in learning.

2. **Directive & Passionate Engagement**
   Teachers need to be directive, influential, caring, and actively and passionately engaged in the process of teaching and learning.

3. **Student-Centric Awareness**
   Teachers need to be aware of what each and every student in their class is thinking and what they know. They must construct meaningful experiences and provide feedback that moves students progressively through the curriculum.

4. **Clarity in Intentions**
   Teachers and students need to know the learning intentions and success criteria. Everyone should know: *Where are you going? How are you going?* and *Where to next?*

5. **Constructing Knowledge**
   Teachers must move from single ideas to multiple related ideas. It is the **learner’s construction** of this knowledge that is critical, not just the ideas themselves.

6. **Welcoming Error**
   School leaders and teachers must create environments where error is welcomed as a learning opportunity. It must be safe to learn, re-learn, and discard incorrect understandings.
