# Six Signposts for Edumacators
### Excellence in Education

The conclusions in *Visible Learning* provide a roadmap for instructional excellence. 
Here are the six signposts:

1. **The Power of Teachers**
   Teachers are among the most powerful influences in learning.

2. **Directive & Passionate Engagement**
   Teachers need to be directive, influential, caring, and actively and passionately engaged in the process of teaching and learning.

3. **Student-Centric Awareness**
   Teachers need to be aware of what each and every student in their class is thinking and what they know. They must construct meaningful experiences and provide feedback that moves students progressively through the curriculum.

4. **Clarity in Intentions**
   Teachers and students need to know the learning intentions and success criteria. Everyone should know: *Where are you going? How are you going?* and *Where to next?*

5. **Constructing Knowledge**
   Teachers must move from single ideas to multiple related ideas. It is the **learner’s construction** of this knowledge that is critical, not just the ideas themselves.

6. **Welcoming Error**
   School leaders and teachers must create environments where error is welcomed as a learning opportunity. It must be safe to learn, re-learn, and discard incorrect understandings.




### Powerful, Passionate, and Accomplished Teachers

Powerful, passionate, and accomplished teachers are those who:  

- **Focus on students’ cognitive engagement** with the content of what is being taught.  
- **Focus on developing ways of thinking and reasoning** that emphasize problem-solving and teaching strategies related to the content they wish students to learn.  
- **Focus on imparting new knowledge and understanding**, and then **monitor how students gain fluency and appreciation** for this new knowledge.  
- **Provide feedback in an appropriate and timely manner** to help students attain the worthwhile goals of the lesson.  
- **Seek feedback** about their own effect on the progress and proficiency of all their students.  
- **Have a deep understanding** about how people learn.  
- **See learning through the eyes of their students**, appreciating their fits and starts in learning, their non-linear progressions toward goals, and  
  - support their deliberate practice,  
  - provide feedback on their errors and misdirections, and  
  - care deeply that students reach the goals and share the teacher’s passion for the material being learned.  

This focus must be **sustained and unrelenting**, and it needs to be **shared by everyone in a school**. 

The above points represent the **foci that drive sustained improvement**. 

Without focus:  

- Even the best leadership ideas will fail.  
- The most ideal research-based initiatives will fail.  
- The most self-sacrificing, earnest leaders will fail.  

Worst of all, **without focused educational leadership**, *students and teachers will fail.*  

*(Reeves, 2011: 14)*
